Sequence tag site (STS)

Gene: STS

Sequence tag sites are small regions of the genome that can be used to understand relationships between individual animals. Sally Lau's study used hundreds of thousands of such sites to build up a picture of genetic relationships going back hundreds of thousands of years

Organism: Turquet's Octopus (Parelelodone turqueti)

Image by David Barnes, British Antarctic Survey

Turquet's octopus is found all around the Antarctic continent

JCU researchers, Dr Sally Lau and Prof. Jan Strugnell led a study that used genetic markers in Turquet's octopus to show that the West Antarctic Ice Sheet most likely collapsed during Earth's last major warm period
