Organism: Southeast Asian liver fluke (Opisthorcis viverrini)

Image by Sripa, et al. (2007). "Liver Fluke Induces Cholangiocarcinoma". PLoS Medicine 4(7): e201. doi:10.1371/journal.pmed.0040201. Cropped from the figure 2.

Opisthorchis viverrini is a trematode worm with a complex lifecycle that requires a mollusc, a fish and a mammalian host at different points of its development. In humans, it infects the bile duct where, over many years, it can cause a type of cancer known as Cholangiocarcinoma.

Researchers at JCU are studying the mechanisms by which this parasitic worm drives cancer development in infected patients. The aim is to develop vaccines that could prevent infection in susceptible populations and to prevent progression to cancer.
